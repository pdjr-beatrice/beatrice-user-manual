3. Vessel design category

The design category is assigned to the boat by the producer of the boat. For your ship, the design category is given on the Declaration of Conformity (DOC), please refer to section 7.

Design categories are defined in the European Boating Directive in the following way:

A. OCEAN

Designed for extended voyages where conditions may exceed wind force 8 (Beaufort scale) and significant wave heights of 4 m and above, and vessels largely self-sufficient.

B. OFFSHORE

Designed for offshore voyages where conditions up to, and including, wind force 8 and significant wave heights up to, and including, 4 m may be experienced.

C. INSHORE

Designed for voyages in coastal waters, large bays, estuaries, lakes and rivers where conditions up to, and including, wind force 6 and significant wave heights up to, and including, 2 m may be experienced.

D. SHELTERED WATERS

Designed for voyages on small lakes, rivers, and canals where conditions up to, and including, wind force 4 and significant wave heights up to, and including, 0,5 m may be experienced.

Your ship is built to exact standards, and complies with the essential requirements of the European Boating Directive which lays down requirements for stability, buoyancy, construction, installations, etc.
