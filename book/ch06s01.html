1. Hauling out / cradling

The vessel can be hauled up or lifted out as long as it is well-supported whilst on land.

Lifting out must be accomplished using two slings of minimum width 15cm. The hull paintwork should be protected from abrasion by putting a soft piece of material between the slings and the hull. Take care with the guard rails: a narrow sling suspension arm can easily squash them if the slings are not threaded through the inside of the rails.

When placing slings ensure that they do not foul thru-hull sensors and the propellor shaft.
